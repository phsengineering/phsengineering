4001A

2017-2018 (In The Zone)

Comprised of juniors, team 4001A has had a prolific track record over the past two competition seasons. During the 2017-2018 game (In the Zone), we captured the Excellence and Tournament Finalist Awards at Sarah Heinz House in Pittsburgh, PA, and later going on to win a Judges Award at Harriton. We then qualified for the Eastern Pennsylvania States Championship, where we captured the Amaze Award, rounding off an impressive rookie season.

2018-2019 Season (Turning Point)

This year, we have started out strong, winning a Judges Award at the Vexmen Open competition in Downingtown, Pennsylvania. As part of our philosophy of improving on our design from last year and fixing the mistakes of the last compeition season, we have designed in a slightly different way, while adhering the principles of engineering design that we learned in class.

Design Process

Flywheel

For the purpose of this website, we decided to examine how we designed our flywheel system for shooting balls at flags in the Vex Turning Point competition. For our flywheel, we used a three motor system that propels the flywheel itself at over 2500 RPM, enabling it to reliably hit the middle and high flags.
